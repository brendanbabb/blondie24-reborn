Blondie24 Reborn

A checkers opponent that evolves while it plays you.

What am I looking at?

A live re-implementation of Chellapilla & Fogel's 1999 evolutionary checkers experiment: a 1,743-weight feedforward network that learns to play checkers with no hand-crafted evaluation, no opening book, no endgame database, and no backpropagation — only self-play under an evolutionary strategy.

Every time the AI moves, it evolves for one second first: its population of 6 networks plays a self-play tournament, the best survives, and that snapshot plays the move against you. Across ~30 AI turns in a single game, the population runs through hundreds of generations — more than the paper's reported 250 for Class-A play — all in the time it takes to play one game.
