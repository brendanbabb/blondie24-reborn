What am I looking at?

This is the companion to the live-evolve demo. Instead of watching a population learn in real time, you play against the finished product: a 2001 "Anaconda" network with 5,048 evolvable weights, trained by pure evolutionary programming for many generations of self-play, then frozen and checked into this repo.

No training happens while you play — the opponent is the fixed output of that offline training run. The AI gets a depth-6 minimax search on every move, so it thinks a little longer than the live-evolve demo's depth-4. That tends to take a second or two per move once the middlegame opens up; endgames are faster.

The model was trained with python -m training.train --preset paper-2001-strict --generations 850 — the strict paper-faithful configuration (μ+μ selection, no quiescence-extension hack). See the repo README for the full deviation / fidelity breakdown and training instructions.

    Network architecture

    The Anaconda 2001 net. 91 overlapping sub-board windows (sizes 3×3 → 8×8) preprocess raw squares, feeding a 92→40→10→1 MLP plus piece-difference and king bypasses.

    Total 5,048 evolvable weights (includes the king weight K — an evolvable scalar that scales king squares to ±K in the input encoding, paper-initialized K=2.0). All initialized from N(0, 0.05) and tuned by evolutionary programming — no backprop, no gradient descent.
